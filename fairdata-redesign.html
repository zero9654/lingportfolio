Faridata Homepage Redesign

Overview

The Problem

Fairdata.fi has not been usability- or accessibility-friendly. The design and information architecture of the website have serious issues affecting all users.

My Role

Design for Web Accessibility, UX/UI Design, Graphic Design

Results

The new design is in the implementation.

My Process

Wireframing -> Prototyping -> Iterative Design -> Final Prototyping

Tool Used

Adobe XD, Pencil & Paper

Timeline

June to August 2020

Home Page Redesign

2D Ilustration for Fairdata services

A proper illustration can easily make user delightful when browsing the website.

Fairdata is a service package including data storage, data publisher, data finder, and data preservation service. It is designed for researchers and research support staff. To provide a better visual experience, the illustration on the right is created to convey the abstract relationships of each service.

3D Ilustration for Fairdata services

3D illustration has been a trend in the design field. It is more concrete than 2D.

The color scheme is the same: blue and green. I applied mainly the metal and glass materials to each component. These two materials have special gloss. After rendering, it gives a strong sense of science and technology.

Information Architecture

Fairdata.fi is a simple website with a broad and shallow structure. It is explicit for users to access each page quickly with two clicks maximum.

The website provides global navigation, local navigation, and sitemaps for supplemental navigation. However, hierarchical categories are not explicitly exclusive due to the labeling confusions such as Fairdata services and Service utilization. Some pages are not placed in reasonable categories, e.g. Fairdata components. These page titles either fail to simply describe the content on the page or overlap with another page title. This labeling problem can affect everyone. Moreover, the website does not provide a search bar to assist users in finding information quickly.

Final Home Page Redesign

Eventually, we use the graph below. 3D illustration obviously doesn't match the same 2D look and feel. While the 2D illustration above, it is too concrete compared with the one below. The one below is merely white towers with research and some datasets. They are consistent and connected in a way but also work independently.

In the original design, blue color takes the whole view. Although blue is a natural color, it is still invasive. It took over the other important information.

Regarding the redesign, the idea is to make the website more visually appealing and emphasize important information. The service name, one-sentence description, and read more links should be obvious to users. I also left more white space. It is important that users can visually breathe when browsing a web page.

The new look is clean and simple. It keeps the original look and feels so existing users will not feel they visit a different website. It is still Fairdata.fi but a better version.

Reflection

There were a lot of communications going on to eventually decide on the home page redesign. It was quite an interesting experience to understand how other people perceive the illustration. Their comments and suggestions have been constructive.

The further steps for Fairdata.fi would be to increase the readability of the online user guides. I will be responsible for reading through user guides and making sure each sentence is easy-to-understand.
