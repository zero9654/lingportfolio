Healer - It is Okay to not be Okay

Overview

The Problem

Finnish red cross youth shelter is a multi-professional community that provides various services. However, those services are quite scattered on multiple platforms. When a user seeks for help, they easily get lost and confused, so that they miss the opportunity to receive our support.

Before the project start, we also get the pemission of doing an investigation there. The manager also indicated that there were plenty of ‘navigation’ problems inside the shopping mall, in which customers have complained a lot.

My Role

Web design, UX/UI design

Results

An integrated AI-powered solution including both web and mobile applications to guide users through all services and eases the huge workload of professionals and volunteers

My Process

Field Study -> Interview -> User Research & Analysis -> Journey Mapping -> Report the findings

Tool Used

Camera, Paper & Pencil, Sketch

Timeline

October to November 2018

Solutions

  1. Apply remarkable lights and decorations at critical parts of REDI, so that customers can rely on these reference objects to navigate.
  2. For bus stops, increasing the coverage by installing rain covers and noise proof barriers.
  3. All signs should follow the same logic and deploy these signs properly. Signs can also place on the floor to guide customers.

Key Findings

We focus on the transport navigation from Redi to the bus stop, and vice versa.

  1. Navigation. Customers get lost quite easily, this has impacted the reputation of REDI.
  2. Bus stop design. Both stops lack cover from either rain or noise.
  3. Redi service point. A lot of info screens, but there is only one info desk. What’s more, there is no clear sign directing customers to the info desk.
  4. Signs. Signs were misleading or missing.

User Research

With a team, we went around inside the building and observing customers. It was quite obvious that there were a lot of customers wandering around to find their way, some were even upset.

Then we randomly interviewed several passengers to get insights from them. 7 out of 14 were first time at REDI as well because of the ‘reputation’ of REDI. They were curious. Some customers were disappointed and expressed very negative feelings about failing to find their way. Few customers were satisfied with REDI since it is new and not many people there. Besides, we also interviewed the info desk and four staff. According to their knowledge and own experience, most of the staff there commute by metro, so none of these staff we interviewed has any idea of the bus stop location.

Qualitative Analysis

With the field study and interview, we have a lot of responses. To sort out these findings, we categorised these as an affinity diagram.

We first captured all keywords from interview transcripts. Then we categorised each answer under the relevant questions. After that, we set three sub-categories as positive feedback, neutral feedback, and negative feedback to get deep insights. Based on our findings and understandings, we discussed and finally came up with the category of ‘novelty of REDI’, ‘visit frequency’, ‘exploration in REDI’, ‘observed forms of transportation’, and ‘negative observations’.

User Journey Map

We found out that the task-oriented customer suffered from indoor navigation problem the most. Thus, we created a customer journey map to visualize each touchpoint along with the changing emotions to find out the key pain point.
