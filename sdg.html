Healer - It is Okay to not be Okay

Overview

The Problem

Finnish red cross youth shelter is a multi-professional community that provides various services. However, those services are quite scattered on multiple platforms. When a user seeks for help, they easily get lost and confused, so that they miss the opportunity to receive our support.

Finnish red crosss looked for a centralised solution to provide them a long-term and consistent support so that they can help as many young people as possible and .

My Role

Web design, UX/UI design

Results

We were the challenge winner. We offer an integrated AI-powered solution including both web and mobile applications to guide users through all services and eases the huge workload of professionals and volunteers. Visit our Healer site here!

Problems

Finnish red cross youth shelter is a multi-professional community that gathers more than 700 volunteers and around 50 employees. Since 2020, they’ve dealt with 1157 cases, held 5911 meetings, and offered accommodation nights to 5813 people in need.

Scattered and disconnected services

The youth shelter provides all sorts of activities from one-to-one chat with mentors, peer groups to discuss or make friends, to digital civic councils. However, those services are quite scattered now. Currently, there is no central channel to gather information, phone calls, and email are the only way to reach out. Besides, no clear guidance is provided about what services are available and how users can utilize them. Due to these reasons, users might get lost during the service journey.

Privacy protection

Normally, young people come to the youth shelter and talk face to face with mentors. Due to the pandemic situation, most services have to transfer to the online environment. So the anonymity and untraceable features become the first priority to consider while choosing the right digital tools. Concerning GDPR and web security, these requirements almost rule out all existing products in the market such as Zoom and teams. Whereas, the only communication tool they use crashes a lot.

On the other hand, although they tried best to ensure the security and privacy. It still inevitably causes trouble to the professionals and volunteers. There are often scenarios where young people have come for help several times but each time they still need to start explaining from the very beginning. This anonymity and untrackable features brings inconvenience and poor experience for young people. It also prevents professionals from providing long-term and persistent mental support. Overall, a balance between privacy protection and (web) connections should be a pain point to be discussed.

Peer pressure

For some young people, they may feel uncomfortable to express their strugglings openly. Based on discussion with mentors, we understood that the huge sucess of Sekasin also thanks to the collaborations with influencers and yle. So, this shall be taken into consideration how we can promote our services smoothly to avoid young people feel pressure or ashamed.

Future vision

The concept of Sanoo booth will first have a pilot testing for a week, and then scale up to the nationwide roadtrip. In the near future, we also have the vision to make the Sanoo booth serve a broader group of people. All residents, no matter what age, would be welcomed to our Sanoo booth to express their feelings and have a warm chat. We also have an idea to expand the Sanoo booth to accommodate a family which is also worth validating through the future pilot testing.

We are always improving, developing, and evolving together with our young people. Our vision is to create an open, warm and secure environment where young people could get their support, parents could get guidance, volunteers could gain information on lending a helping hand, and partners and donors could also get access to realize their value.

User Journey Map

We found out that the task-oriented customer suffered from indoor navigation problem the most. Thus, we created a customer journey map to visualize each touchpoint along with the changing emotions to find out the key pain point.

Pre-arrival

  1. Redi official website: The official website of Redi contains sufficient information about Redi. Laura searches the location of the cafeteria through the floor map providing online.

Arrival

  1. Redi guide signs: There is a huge number of Signs placing at Redi to guide people. Laura tries to find the cafe by following these signs.
  2. Info Screen: It is an interactive touch screen, which provides sufficient information on the shopping mall, including discounts, floor map, stores, and brands. There are several info screens installed in different places at Redi. Laura uses a info screen for finding her way to the cafe.

Departure

  1. Info desk: There is only one info desk in the shopping mall, which is deployed to provide customer information and support related to the Redi mall. Laura asks info desk for navigating to the bus stop.
